Activity 8: Radio Watch Duty

Learn radio communication with real vessel examples

Read the situation carefully. Choose your role — caller or receiver — and perform the message aloud using the correct maritime alphabet and number pronunciation. You may switch roles to practice both sides.

Situation 1

Late at night, MV CORALIS (MMSI: 538209100, Call Sign: CLFW2) is sailing through the calm waters of the Lombok Strait. On the radar, another vessel appears — MV SILVERWIND (MMSI: 312985600, Call Sign: SVNP4) — moving in the opposite direction.

To ensure safe passage, the captain of Coralis decides to make a short radio call to check whether the channel is clear before crossing paths.
